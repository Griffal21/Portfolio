About Me

Upon graduating from the University of Scranton with a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics I took a gap year in my studies where I primarily tutored High School and Middle School Math, Science, Social Studies, and Standardized Exam Prep. During this time I also earned a trade school degree as an automotive technician, as mechanical restoration and do it yourself repair has always been a passion of mine. My studies resumed the following year with the goal of becoming a secondary mathematics teacher. During such time I also took a job at Six Flags Great Adventure as a Unit Supervisor, Staffing Supervisor, and Labor Analyst. While there I earned a reputation of someone who was able to solve tough problems, motivate workers initially unwilling to work hard, effectively mediate problems between customers and employees, and someone who enforced and followed policies. I excelled at Rider University until I reached the step of student teaching and I realized teaching was not for me. I then enrolled in Rutgers University's coding bootcamp to become fluent in multiple coding languages. It was here I learned HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, jQuery, Ajax, React.js, and MySQL just to name a few.

Now that I have graduated I'm now seeking a career in the workforce that will utilize my mathematical, problem sovling, and computer programming skills.
